Google Home for Alzheimer's

UX Research Plan

Challenge

A case study assumes that I, as a researcher from Google, am working with the Alzheimer's Association to research how Google Home features can support people with Alzheimer's in managing their daily activities and relationships with their loved ones.

The research activities are planned on a four-week timeline, and then you need to demonstrate the potential of Google Home to help and improve the quality of life of people with Alzheimer's.

And the task is to plan a study to solve the challenge.

  1. Read carefully a case study, and based on case study
  2. Develop research objectives and questions.
  3. Make a research hypothesis.
  4. Determine the research method.
  5. Develop a research timeline.

Research Objective

  1. How Google Home can manage the daily activities of people with Alzheimer's?
  2. Does the control system in google home help well?
  3. What features should be built into Google Home to meet the needs of people with Alzheimer's?
  4. Find out how to provide the best experience for people with Alzheimer's.

Research Questions

Functions:

  1. Does the Google Home service fully help Alzheimer's sufferers?
  2. Is the Google Home application easy to use?

Feature:

  1. Do the features provided meet all the needs of people with Alzheimer's?
  2. What features are often used by people with Alzheimer's?

Intractions:

  1. Does the interaction with google home give a good response?
  2. Is the audio provided easy to understand?

Research Hypothesis

  1. We believe develop the sensitivity of recording and capturing sound on Google Home, because people with Alzheimer's are one of them relatively sensitive to pronunciation.
  2. We believe adding service features that apply offline to Google Home can help people with Alzheimer's, because Google Home still relies on a stable internet connection.
  3. We believe taking a conventional approach where Google Home dominates in interaction can increase comfort in communicating, because sufferers, especially the elderly, are sensitive to changes in mood and behavior.

Research Method

Explore the needs of users with Alzheimer's, to provide the best experience for users when using Google Home in carrying out their daily activities.

Interview

  1. Helps me gain a deep understanding of users' needs, wants, and problems. By talking directly to users, I am able to capture nuances and context that are not possible from other data or analysis.
  2. Through one-on-one interactions with users, I can mine specific and in-depth information. I can ask detailed questions and dig for answers that provide valuable insights into how users interact with your product or service.
  3. Helps in validating design assumptions or product ideas. I can confirm or debunk my assumptions by hearing directly from users.

Shadowing

  1. Following users in their daily activities. It provides first-hand insights into the user context, the challenges they face, and how they use your product or service in real life.
  2. I can catch problems or difficulties that you may not have thought of before. This helps you refine assumptions and improve your product design to better suit user needs.
  3. I can see the world from the user's perspective. This helps me design more relevant and meaningful experiences for them.
