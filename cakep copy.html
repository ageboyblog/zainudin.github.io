CakeP

Case Study

This project is one of my challenges when attending the bootcamp at binar academy. I chose a case study, namely "A cake shop that starts to have customers who are lazy to leave the house and want delivery". I chose this case because it represents society and me at this time because of the development of technology as it is today.

People's shopping style is starting to shift from conventional to digital or online, especially triggered by conditions like today, this can be beneficial for consumers, one of which can save their energy and time.

My Role

UI Design, UX Research, Logo Design

Tools

Figma, Whimsical, Google Workspace, Zoom

Timeline

2 Days

Usability Testing Plan

The purpose of this plan is to document what I will do, how I will conduct the test, what metrics will be captured, the number of participants to test, and what scenarios I will use.

Concept

In this usability testing session, I chose to use Guerilla Testing and utilize Matrix Of Measurement as a usability assessment. The selection of this method is based on its effectiveness in minimizing time, with strict time constraints. In addition, this approach is relatively easy and simple for me to learn. For this testing session, I chose the Moderate Usability Testing method because it was deemed suitable for gaining an in-depth understanding of the user experience, allowing for more detailed questioning and immediate feedback.

Participants

In the process of selecting participants for this Usability Testing, they were chosen based on their level of experience in using similar products. Through this approach, I hoped to gain insight into the range of user responses and experiences to the product I was testing.

Scenario Plan

Created several scenarios that will be carried out by the participants, this aims to be able to measure the complexity when a flow that the user wants to achieve.

UT Results

To filter the test results, is to document the task completion of each test taker.

Feedback

  1. Color placement in the interface (such as app background)
  2. Button on item selection in the cart is difficult to reach
  3. Difficult to distinguish the status of items
  4. There is a font that is too small
  5. When the user has bought a cake, a notification will appear in the notification

Improvement

  1. Organize the flow when according to the cake purchase
  2. Improve the hierarchy of writing title, subtitle, and body
  3. Redesign the cart layout to make the item selection button easier to reach
  4. Improve the clarity of item status by adding clear visual indicators, such as icons or color codes
  5. Ensure adequate contrast between text and background
